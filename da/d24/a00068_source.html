3 /**
4  * Class to represent a three dimensional vector.
5  *
6  * <p>
7  * It is defined as a point <i>(x,y,z)</i> in three dimensional
8  * vector space. It is stored as a array of <code>float</code>s.
9  * The three dimensions are known as X,Y and Z.
10  * </p>
11  * <p>
12  * <b>Dot product of two vectors : </b> <br/>
13  * <i>
14  * (a<sub>1</sub>,b<sub>1</sub>,c<sub>1</sub>) .
15  * (a<sub>2</sub>,b<sub>2</sub>,c<sub>2</sub>) =
16  * a<sub>1</sub>a<sub>2</sub> + b<sub>1</sub>b<sub>2</sub> +
17  * c<sub>1</sub>c<sub>2</sub>
18  * </i>.
19  * </p>
20  * <p>
21  * <b>Product of a vector with a scalar : </b> <br/>
22  * <i>(a, b, c) * s = (a * s, b * s, c * s)</i>.
23  * </p>
24  * <p>
25  * <b>Normalization of a vector</b> <i>(a,b,c)</i> <br/>
26  * &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
27  * = <i>(a/mag, b/mag, c/mag)</i>, <br/>
28  * where, <br/> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
29  * <i>mag =
30  * &radic;(a<sup>2</sup>+b<sup>2</sup>+c<sup>2</sup>)</i>.
31  * </p>
32  *
33  * @see project.android.sensor.representation.Vector3f
34  * @see project.android.sensor.representation.Quaternion
35  */
